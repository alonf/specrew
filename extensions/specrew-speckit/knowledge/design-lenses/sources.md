the source materials.

- Introduction: book structure and architecture-as-practice framing.
- Chapter 1: software product, architecture goals, stakeholders, process,
  estimation, constraints, volatility, coupling, and cohesion.
- Chapter 2: requirements, personas, NFRs, constraints, discovery workshops,
  SRS, use cases, user stories, story mapping, prototypes, and sketches.
- Chapter 3: analysis frameworks, viewpoints, perspectives, DDD, volatility,
  layers, cross-cutting concerns, managers, engines, accessors, request flow,
  shaping architecture, high-level design, and risk mitigation.

  SDKs, static/dynamic views, SOLID, package principles, and extensibility.
- Chapter 6: cross-cutting concerns, hosting, configuration, secrets, DevOps,

  metrics, tracing, alerting, error handling, retries, idempotency, and recovery.
